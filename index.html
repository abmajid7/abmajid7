I am Abdul Majid, a first-year Bachelor of Library and Information Science (B.Lib.I.Sc.) student at the prestigious Jamia Millia Islamia, New Delhi. My academic journey began at Government Degree College Poonch, Jammu University, where I completed my graduation.

Growing up surrounded by mountains in Poonch, Jammu & Kashmir, I developed a love for books and stories early on. This passion evolved into a desire to understand how knowledge is organised, preserved, and made accessible to everyone — irrespective of their background.

I aspire to modernise library systems by integrating digital tools with traditional cataloguing principles, making information equitable and accessible in rural and underserved communities like the one I come from.

06 — Core Beliefs

Ranganathan's Five Laws

My professional philosophy is rooted in Dr. S.R. Ranganathan's timeless Five Laws of Library Science — the ethical compass of every librarian.

1
Books are for Use
Libraries exist to serve people — not as museums. Every resource must be made actively accessible, not preserved behind barriers.
2
Every Book Its Reader
Each piece of knowledge has someone who needs it. The librarian's mission is to make that connection happen, for everyone.
3
Every Reader Their Book
Every person who walks into a library deserves to find what they are looking for — no one should leave empty-handed.
4
Save the Time of the Reader
Good cataloguing, intuitive systems, and responsive service are acts of respect for the most precious resource — time.
5
A Library is a Growing Organism
Libraries must evolve — in collections, technology, and service models. Stagnation is antithetical to the mission of knowledge.
